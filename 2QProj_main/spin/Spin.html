SPIN: the deadly hidden side of Table Tennis

In the early of table tennis most players used a traditional "hardbat" which is consisted of pimpled rubber, without sponge, on a wooden blade. This entails that the game soley relies on ball placement and control. After the introduction of the sponge in the 1950s, it became possible to impart spin on the ball. The use of spin is now the most dominant factor in the sport. For anyone who wants to improve their game, being able to recieve and strike a spin is an important skill to master.

The basics of spin

practice makes perfect

no player can immediately learn spin. It is a skill that relies on constant practice and improvement.

  1. When you hit the ball near its center with your racket at a 90 degree angle, the ball will travel forward more and have less to no spin.
  3. When you brush the ball with your racket at an angle of less than or more than 90 degrees (using an upward, sideward, or downward movement) the ball will spin more and not travel as far forward.




Top Spin

Topspin is produced by starting your stroke below the ball and brushing your racket tangentially against the ball in an UPWARD motion. The faster the stroke, the more spin. When top spin is implied the ball stays low and accelerates forward.

Backspin

Backspin is produced by starting your stroke above the ball and brushing your racket tangentially against the ball in a DOWNWARD motion. When backspin is implied, the ball will rise up more and not travel as far.

Side Spin

Side spin is produced by starting your stroke above the ball and brushing your racket tangentially against the ball in a SIDEWAYS motion. When you impart left side spin on the ball, it will move to the right - and vice versa.
