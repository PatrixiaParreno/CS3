Basic Strokes

There are two (2) basic strokes in table tennis - forehand and backhand. Mastering these two strokes is essential in improving a player's game. These are the core fundamentals of table tennis, and perfecting these strokes can make your game improve by a large margin. Being able to control the ball is a huge advantage for beginners. By learning how to control the ball, you can plan specific attacks to help beat your opponent.

Forehand

Forehand is a stroke played with the hand facing in the direction of the stroke. It is an offensive stroke that is used to force errors upon your opponent, and to set up attacking positions.

Backhand

Backhand is a stroke played with the back side of the hand facing in the direction of the stroke, with the arm across the body.
